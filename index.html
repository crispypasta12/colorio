Color Match AI

Included in LUT Exports

Match the color, brightness and contrast of a reference image to your current image with Color Match AI in Color.io. Ideal for quickly replicating film looks, creative references, or just to kickstart inspiration for your next color grade.

  • Import Reference: Click the folder icon or drag and drop custom reference images.
  • Match Process: Click the reference thumbnail to initiate matching. Two sliders (Color and Tone) appear for fine-tuning.
  • Color Slider: Adjusting Color will affect how much of the hue and saturation are matched.
  • Tone Slider: Adjusting Tone affects how much the brightness and contrast are matched.
  • Color Management: When you change the Input Color Space after matching, you should re-generate the match to keep it aligned with your image.

Pro Tip: For the highest quality color transfer, use uncompressed PNG reference images.

 Balance

Balance

Included in LUT Exports

Adjust global exposure, saturation, temperature, and tint with the 2-Axis controls in the Balance Panel.

  • Exposure/Saturation Control: Y-axis adjusts exposure; X-axis adjusts saturation.
  • Tint/Temperature Control: Y-axis adjusts tint; X-axis adjusts color temperature.
  • Keyboard Modifiers: Hold Alt/Option to lock a point to the Y-Axis. Hold Shift to lock on X
  • Reset: Double-click the control point to reset values.

Film Derived Transforms:Exposure uses non-linear shapers to protect shadows and highlights from clipping. Saturation models analog film density and temperature is a physically based CTO / CTB emulation.

 Exposure Curve

Exposure Curve

Included in LUT Exports

The Exposure Curve allows you to shape brightness across shadows, midtones, and highlights with precise control over luminance ranges.

  • Curve: Drag points to brighten (up) or darken (down) specific tonal ranges based on image luminance.
  • Master Slider: Move the entire curve globally like a regular exposure control.
  • Point Control: Adjust between 2 and 7 points. Smart interpolation maintains curve shape.
  • Interpolation Modes: Cycle between Cubic, Bezier, and Linear for different curve smoothness.
  • Keyboard Shortcuts: Cmd/Ctrl + Drag locks movement to the Y-axis. Double-click a point to reset it.

Pro Tip: Shape exposure and dynamic range creatively by brightening shadows without affecting highlights, or flattening midtones for cinematic looks.

 Contrast Curve

Contrast Curve

Included in LUT Exports

Adjust image contrast distribution across tonal zones with full control over curve shape and interpolation. The contrast curve is filmic by default via built-in brightness-dependent film density modeling: Darkening colors increases their density and brightening exhibits a subtle bleach effect.

  • Curve Editor: Drag points to control shadow, midtone, and highlight contrast.
  • Smart Contrast Slider: Temporarily applies positive (S-curve) or negative contrast (inverted S-curve).
  • Point Count: Adjust between 2 and 7 control points with smooth redistribution.
  • Interpolation Modes: Switch between Cubic, Bezier, and Linear curves.
  • Keyboard Shortcuts: Double-click to reset points. Cmd/Ctrl + Drag locks to Y-axis adjustment.

Pro Tip: Use the Smart Contrast slider on the left to create fast, elegant S-curves for filmic contrast adjustments.

 Scattering

Scattering

Included in LUT Exports

Add physically accurate shadow and highlight tinting based on natural light behavior with the color wheels in the Scattering panel. Ideal for creating film-like, cinematic color contrasts.

  • Shadow Wheel: Add ambient color to the shadows of an image. Unlike traditional color wheels, scattering protects naturally occurring shadow colors in an image and tints dark pixels in a way that is consistent with light behaviour in the physical world.
  • Highlight Wheel: Tints bright areas, simulating direct light. The model is optimized to prevent highlight spill into the darker range of the image which is typical of traditional lift gamma gain controls.
  • Control: Drag within each wheel to adjust tint hue (direction) and intensity (distance from center).
  • Shortcuts: Cmd/Ctrl + Drag locks the hue angle for intensity-only adjustments. Shift + Drag locks the intensity for hue-only adjustments.
  • Reset: Double-click control points to reset them to neutral.

Pro Tip: Create natural cinematic looks by pairing cool shadows with warm highlights, mimicking the color separation found in print film stocks.

 Refraction

Refraction

Included in LUT Exports

AKA Filmic HSL. Refraction wheels are a power-tool for filmic color remapping and compression. Change hue and saturation in shadows and highlights independently for all color vectors using a highly stable, filmic color transform model.

  • Refraction Wheels: Adjust hue and film density of six primary color points (Red, Yellow, Green, Cyan, Blue, Magenta) separately for shadows and highlights.
  • Link/Unlink: Control both luminance regions together or separately for shadows vs highlights.
  • Threshold: Adjust where the shadow-to-highlight split occurs.
  • Shortcuts: Cmd/Ctrl + Drag locks the hue angle for density-only adjustments. Shift + Drag locks the density for hue-only adjustments.
  • Reset: Double-click individual points or use the panel reset button.

Built in Film Emulation: Color Refraction provides advanced film-like color compression and expansion capabilities, with built-in film density for fine grained color control over the LUT or image. Move points closer to each other to perform hue-compression, or spread them out to increase color contrast.

Density Curve

Density Curve

Included in LUT Exports

Control color richness and saturation hue-by-hue with the Density Curve. Based on analog film density behavior, it deepens colors as saturation increases.

  • Hue-Based Curve: Adjust Red, Yellow, Green, Cyan, Blue, and Magenta individually.
  • Master Slider: Global adjustment of overall color density across all hues.
  • Point Count: Choose between 2–7 points, smart redistribution maintains curve shape.
  • Interpolation Modes: Switch between Bezier (default), Linear, or Cubic.
  • Shortcut: Alt/Option + Drag to lock a point to the current hue.
  • Reset: Double-click a point to reset or use the panel reset button.

Pro Tip: Deepen skies, desaturate skin tones, and selectively enhance colors across the spectrum with painterly, filmic color and without risking oversaturation.

 Chroma Curve

Chroma Curve

Included in LUT Exports

Adjust saturation based on how colorful pixels already are. Use the Chroma Curve to selectively boost muted tones or protect vibrant highlights.

  • Chrominance-Based Curve: Left = Grayscale tones, Right = Highly saturated areas.
  • Master Slider: Shifts density globally across all chroma levels.
  • Point Count: Adjust 2–7 points with smart redistribution.
  • Interpolation Modes: Cycle between Cubic (default), Bezier, or Linear curves.
  • Reset: Double-click a point to reset it to neutral.

Pro Tip: Push the left side of the curve up to enhance muted colors, and pull the right side down to keep vibrant colors in check. This creates organic, balanced saturation.

 Radiance Curve

Radiance Curve

Included in LUT Exports

Adjust brightness per hue using the Radiance Curve. Ideal for subtle tonal balancing and enhancing color contrast naturally without masking.

  • Hue-Based Curve: Red, Yellow, Green, Cyan, Blue, and Magenta hue points.
  • Master Slider: Global brightness adjustment across all hues.
  • Point Count: 2–7 points, smart redistribution.
  • Interpolation Modes: Switch between Bezier (default), Linear, or Cubic.

Pro Tip: Brighten foliage selectively, darken luminous skies, and separate hues tonally for refined, filmic depth.

 Saturation Curve

Saturation Curve

Included in LUT Exports

Control saturation across different brightness ranges. Boost saturation in shadows or reduce saturation in highlights to avoid color clipping.

  • Luminance-Based Curve: Left = Shadows, Right = Highlights.
  • Master Slider: Move all curve points in unison for global saturation adjustment.
  • Point Count: Adjust between 2–7 points.
  • Interpolation Modes: Switch between Bezier (default), Linear, or Cubic curves.
  • Keyboard Shortcuts: Cmd/Ctrl + Drag locks movement to Y-axis. Double-click to reset points.

Pro Tip: Boost color richness in shadows or desaturate highlights to keep them clean and prevent chroma clipping in skies and lights.

 Shadow Highlight

Shadows & Highlights

Included in LUT Exports

Adjust brightness and introduce subtle hue shifts separately in the shadows and highlights of your image for fine tonal and color control.

  • Shadows Section: Three RGB sliders to brighten, darken, or add color offsets to shadow areas.
  • Highlights Section: Three RGB sliders to brighten, darken, or add color offsets to highlights.
  • Linked Sliders: Move together to adjust brightness neutrally. Unlink to introduce color shifts.
  • Quick Rebalance: Cmd/Ctrl + Click on a slider (when unlinked) averages them back to neutral without resetting brightness.
  • Reset: Double-click any slider to reset it to zero.

Pro Tip: Introduce cinematic color contrast by cooling shadows slightly and warming highlights subtly for natural tonal separation.

 SpotlightFX

Spotlight

Render-Only (Not Included in LUTs)

Spotlight allows dynamic re-illumination of the image by applying localized exposure and pop, enhancing three-dimensional depth and mood.

  • Spotlight Amount: Controls the intensity of the relighting effect.
  • Pop: Enhances definition in the focus area.
  • Bias: ← Left only darkens the background. Right → only brightens the subject. Center is balanced.
  • Focus: Narrows (→) or widens (←) the focus area where relighting is applied to the image.
  • Center: Move the center of the focus area to highlight different parts of the frame.

When to use: Use Spotlight subtly to restore subject prominence, enhance perceived depth in flat images or add a vignette.

 HalationFX

Halation

Render-Only (Not Included in LUTs)

Simulate the iconic red-orange glow around highlights seen in analog film photography using physically modeled halation emulation.

  • Halation Amount: Drag the knob right to increase the glow around bright areas.
  • Light Spill: Expands halation into midtones and darker regions.
  • Color Shift: Adjust halation hue between magenta, red, and yellow.
  • Saturation: Control how colorful the halation glow appears.

Good to know: For accurate in-app preview, always evaluate halation at 100% zoom level. Zooming out can reduce subtle bright parts of the image that normally emit halation.

 DiffusionFX

Diffusion

Render-Only (Not Included in LUTs)

Apply real-time optical diffusion that softens highlights, blooms bright areas, and maintains subject focus — inspired by real-world diffusion filters.

  • Diffusion Amount: Main knob controlling the strength of bloom and softness.
  • Fog: Lifts shadow blacks slightly, enhancing atmospheric depth.
  • Threshold: Restricts diffusion to only affect highlights, protecting shadow sharpness.
  • Focus: Protects the center of the image from diffusion for sharper subjects.
  • Focus Point Selector: Move the protected center focus point anywhere in the frame.

Good to know: For accurate in-app preview, always evaluate diffusion at 100% zoom level. Zooming out can reduce bright parts of the image which affects the simulated diffusion radius.

 TextureFX

Texture

Render-Only (Not Included in LUTs)

Re-texture every pixel of digital images with volumetric film grain and localized micro-contrast. Simulate anything from fine-grained 35mm film to gritty ISO 800 stocks.

  • Film Grain Amount: Controls the visibility and strength of the film grain simulation.
  • Grain Chroma: Adjusts how colorful or monochromatic the grain appears.
  • Film Acutance: Enhances filmic blacks around high density areas for realistic, localized film contrast emulation.
  • Film Resolution: Perceptual, contrast-adaptive image sharpening (or softening). Influences grain roughness.

Pro Tip: For an authentic analog film scan look, use medium grain, slightly boosted accutance, and slightly soft film resolution settings.
